Science Lesson 1: The Cell
The cell is the basic structural, functional, and biological unit of all known organisms. It is the smallest unit of life. Cells are often called the "building blocks of life."
Science Lesson 2: Chemical Reactions
A chemical reaction is a process that leads to the chemical transformation of one set of chemical substances to another. Chemical reactions are everywhere, from the cooking of food to the rusting of iron.
Science Lesson 3: Newton's Laws
Sir Isaac Newton's three laws of motion describe the relationship between a body and the forces acting upon it, and its motion in response to those forces. They form the basis of classical mechanics.
Science Lesson 4: Photosynthesis
Photosynthesis is the process used by plants, algae, and certain bacteria to convert light energy into chemical energy, which fuels the organism's activities.
Science Lesson 5: Gravity & Space
Gravity is a fundamental force of nature that attracts masses towards each other. It is what keeps planets in orbit around stars, and stars grouped into galaxies.
Social Lesson 1: Indian Constitution
The Constitution of India is the supreme law of India. It lays down the framework defining fundamental political principles, structure, procedures, powers and duties of government institutions.
Social Lesson 2: Mughal Empire
The Mughal Empire was an early modern empire that controlled much of South Asia between the 16th and 19th centuries. It was known for its grand architecture and culture.
Social Lesson 3: Geography of Rivers
Rivers play a vital role in shaping the geography of a region, providing fertile land, water resources, and historical trade routes.
Social Lesson 4: Democracy
Democracy is a system of government where the citizens exercise power, either directly or by electing representatives from among themselves to form a governing body.
Social Lesson 5: World Wars
The two World Wars of the 20th century reshaped the global political map and led to the creation of international bodies designed to promote peace and cooperation.
English Lesson 1: The Passive Voice
The passive voice is a grammatical construction where the subject of the sentence is the recipient of the action rather than the performer of the action. Example: "The ball was thrown by the boy."
English Lesson 2: Poetry Analysis
Analyzing poetry involves examining its form, structure, rhythm, rhyme scheme, and literary devices (like metaphors and similes) to understand the poet's message.
English Lesson 3: Formal Letter Writing
Formal letters are used for official purposes, such as communicating with businesses, government departments, or school authorities. They require a specific, structured format.
English Lesson 4: Short Story: The Gift
This lesson explores a classic short story, focusing on character development, plot twists, and underlying themes like sacrifice and love.
English Lesson 5: Reported Speech
Reported speech (or indirect speech) is how we tell someone what another person said without using the exact words. Tense changes and shifts in pronouns are necessary.
Maths Lesson 1: Algebraic Identities
Algebraic identities are equations that are true for all values of the variables. Key identities include $(a+b)^2$, $(a-b)^2$, and $(a^2 - b^2)$.
Maths Lesson 2: Trigonometry Basics
Trigonometry deals with the relationships between the sides and angles of triangles. The primary functions are sine, cosine, and tangent.
Maths Lesson 3: Quadratic Equations
A quadratic equation is a second-degree polynomial equation in a single variable $x$, where $a \ne 0$. The general form is $ax^2 + bx + c = 0$.
Maths Lesson 4: Probability
Probability is the measure of the likelihood that an event will occur. It is quantified as a number between 0 and 1, where 0 indicates impossibility and 1 indicates certainty.
Maths Lesson 5: Mensuration
Mensuration is the branch of geometry that is concerned with the measurement of lengths, areas, and volumes of geometric figures, such as circles, spheres, and prisms.
